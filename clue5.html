A quiet walk, the garden dim and alive.
The air knew something we didn’t yet.
I said I needed a shower. You sat on the bed, watching.
Not just my body, but my being.
And in your smile, I felt something I never had before —
not desire, not lust, but love.
You didn’t look away. You saw me.
And I think that was the moment I let you.
That night wasn’t perfect. But it was honest.
And that made it unforgettable.
The restaurant was closed. But we had the world in a bed
with plastic cutlery and laughter.
I thought you weren’t hungry. I worried.
But you’d already gone, eaten alone, and come back to hold me again.
That’s love too — quiet, guilty, sweet.
